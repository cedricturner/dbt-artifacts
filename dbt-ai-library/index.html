dbt's AI roadmap

The dbt AI Library

For a decade, dbt has been how data teams make analytics trustworthy: every transformation tested, documented, and governed. The hard part was never running the SQL. It was being able to trust the number at the end.

AI changes who reads that number. The consumer is no longer just a dashboard, it is an agent that reasons and acts on your data. Agents are fast but unpredictable, and they need exactly what analysts always needed: a foundation they can trust. That is where dbt is headed, to be the open, trusted foundation that agents are built on, owned by you rather than rented from whoever sells the compute. The model is not the differentiator. The foundation is.

The rest of this page is about understanding how that foundation comes together, one layer at a time. To keep it concrete, I will walk through it as a library.

IN THE LIBRARY

This is the library before anyone organized it. Every book is on a shelf somewhere, all your models, tests, and metrics, but there are no index cards and no staff. To answer a question you wander the stacks, pull whatever looks close, and read it cover to cover. You might grab the definitive edition, or a draft someone abandoned, and nothing on the shelf tells you which is which.

IN THE LIBRARY

Now the library has a card catalog. Every book gets an index card: its subject, its exact shelf, and the other books it references. You walk up, flip to the card, and go straight to the right shelf, no wandering and no wrong editions. But the catalog is still just cards. It tells you where the book is, not whether you read it correctly, and it never reads the book for you.

MissingAnyone to confirm the answer is right, or to show where it came from.

IN THE LIBRARY

Now there is a reference desk. You can take any book to the desk, and a careful librarian checks it against the catalog, tells you how confident they are, notes which edition it came from, and stamps the record. If two sources disagree, they catch it. You are no longer trusting your own reading, you are leaving with an answer the library itself vouches for.

MissingYou still do the asking, the lookups, and the legwork yourself.

IN THE LIBRARY

Now the librarian comes to you. You never touch the index cards or carry books to the desk. You simply say what you need, and they find the right book, check it at the desk, and hand you the answer already verified, and will even reshelve a new volume for you. The whole library, the books, the cards, and the desk, working on your behalf from a single question.

Your personal dbt agent, wherever you work.
Context→Trust→Experience

Same library, four ways to walk in. The books hold the knowledge, the catalog makes it findable, the desk makes it trustworthy, and the librarian makes it effortless. Each layer stands on its own, and each one makes the next more powerful. The foundation dbt built for the BI era, extended for the age of agents.
